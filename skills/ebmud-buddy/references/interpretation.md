# EBMUD Buddy Interpretation Notes

## Primary Signals

- Rising winter/spring GPD usually points to indoor baseline changes: occupancy, family size, laundry, showers/baths, toilet flushing, dishwashing, guests, or indoor leaks.
- Rising summer/fall GPD usually points to outdoor demand: irrigation schedules, dry weather, establishment watering, overspray, valve leaks, pressure issues, or runoff.
- Fall staying high after summer often means irrigation schedules are carrying forward, landscape demand remains high, or a broken system is wasting water.
- A single high WaterScore period in March or winter deserves a cheap leak/controller check before assuming it is normal.

## Benchmarks

EBMUD's `Average Households GPD` and `Top 20% GPD` columns are useful context, but they shouldn't be the main diagnostic target. Neighborhood averages can be very high during irrigation season. So a home can look "average" while still rising sharply against its own history.

## Baseline Reasoning

When household context changed, estimate the current baseline from recent winter/spring periods rather than the lowest historical periods. For a larger or full-time occupied household, older low-use months can be misleading.

Use baseline bands rather than false precision:

- Lower-occupancy household: often near the historical winter/spring median.
- Full-time occupied household with kids: recent winter/spring average is often more realistic.
- Frequent flushing or older toilets can move baseline by tens of gallons per day.

## Recommended Checks

Start with fast diagnostics:

- Toilet dye tests for silent flapper/fill-valve leaks.
- Meter test with all fixtures and irrigation off.
- Controller audit: days/week, runtime, seasonal adjustment, rain skip, and fall carryover.
- Irrigation walk-through during active zones: overspray, runoff, broken emitters, misting, weak far-end coverage.

For yard redesign:

- Design irrigation and planting together.
- Use hydrozones by plant water need.
- Prefer drip for beds and shrubs.
- Add pressure regulation, check valves, smart controller, and flow monitoring where practical.
- Expect first-year establishment watering, then watch for the seasonal lift to taper.
