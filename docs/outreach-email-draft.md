# Outreach Email Draft

Subject: Independent browser-local prototype for helping EBMUD customers understand usage exports

Hi Mr. Chan,

I'm Dan O'Leary, an EBMUD customer in Lafayette. I built a small browser-local prototype called Mud Buddy that helps EBMUD customers understand their own exported water-usage file.

The idea is simple: a customer downloads their EBMUD usage export, opens Mud Buddy, and gets a private plain-English report about what changed, what may be worth checking, and when to use official EBMUD resources.

I designed the prototype with conservative boundaries:

- no EBMUD password handling;
- no credential automation;
- no CSV upload to a Mud Buddy server;
- no account, billing, outage, water-quality, rebate, assistance, or emergency action;
- clear non-affiliation language;
- official EBMUD links for official next steps.

This is not presented as a leak detector, billing tool, formal audit, or official EBMUD analysis. It is a customer education and usage-interpretation prototype.

I would value EBMUD's feedback on whether this kind of browser-local tool could help customers understand high bills, irrigation-season usage, fixture checks, and conservation opportunities. If useful, I would be happy to explore a review or pilot path with the appropriate EBMUD team.

Live prototype: https://danieloleary.github.io/mud-buddy/

GitHub repo: https://github.com/danieloleary/mud-buddy

Thank you,

Dan O'Leary
