# Gallon Savings Methodology

Mud Buddy's public mission is to help East Bay households save money and find **millions of gallons of potential water savings**, starting with the first milestone of **1 million gallons**.

This is a rallying goal and product-impact estimate, not a verified EBMUD conservation total.

## Source scale

- EBMUD says its water system serves about 1.4 million people across a 332-square-mile service area.
- EBMUD rate materials define `1 CCF` as `748 gallons`.

Sources:

- [EBMUD service area](https://www.ebmud.com/about-us/who-we-are/service-area)
- [EBMUD FY2026 rate document](https://www.ebmud.com/download_file/force/34400/702?Rate_Document_for_FY_2026_Web.pdf=)

## Goal math

- First milestone: `1,000,000 gallons / 748 gallons per CCF = ~1,337 CCF`.
- `1 acre-foot = 325,851 gallons`, so `1,000,000 gallons = ~3.1 acre-feet`.
- Example community path: `200 households x 5,000 gallons = 1,000,000 gallons`.

## What counts as helped-save potential

Mud Buddy may count potential savings when a user reports or documents an action that plausibly reduces future water use, such as:

- Finding and fixing an irrigation schedule issue.
- Running a toilet dye test and fixing a running toilet.
- Performing a meter test after seeing normal use slowly rise.
- Finding an irrigation leak or stuck zone.
- Adjusting landscape watering after seeing likely outdoor watering increase.
- Understanding that normal household use changed and setting a new conservation target.

## What Mud Buddy must not claim

- Mud Buddy does not certify actual water savings.
- Mud Buddy does not diagnose leaks.
- Mud Buddy does not inspect plumbing or irrigation systems.
- Mud Buddy does not provide billing advice or official utility analysis.
- Mud Buddy is not affiliated with EBMUD.

The safest wording is `helped-save`, `potential savings`, or `patterns worth checking`.

Baseline, outdoor-watering, and average-household benchmark labels are heuristic pattern clues. They should not be counted as verified savings, official classifications, normalized customer comparisons, or certified conservation results.
