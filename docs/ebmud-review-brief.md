# Mud Buddy Review Brief

Mud Buddy is an independent browser-local prototype for EBMUD customers. It helps a customer upload an exported EBMUD usage CSV, see a plain-English water-use report, and understand practical next checks.

Mud Buddy is not affiliated with, endorsed by, approved by, or officially reviewed by EBMUD unless EBMUD explicitly says so in writing.

## 30-second summary

- Product: private browser-local EBMUD CSV analyzer.
- Audience: EBMUD customers who want to understand high bills, outdoor watering, normal daily use changes, and patterns worth checking.
- Privacy model: the public app reads the selected CSV in the browser and does not upload it to a Mud Buddy server.
- Safety model: Mud Buddy does not ask for credentials, enter MFA, change account settings, diagnose leaks, decide bills, or replace official EBMUD resources.
- Collaboration ask: review the prototype, privacy model, methodology language, and whether a customer-education or conservation pilot would be useful.

## Customer benefit

Many customers can download usage data but still struggle to understand what changed. Mud Buddy translates that export into:

- a normal daily use estimate;
- likely outdoor watering clues;
- highest-use billing period;
- CSV/data-quality notes;
- recommended next steps;
- official EBMUD resource routing.

## Boundaries

Mud Buddy helps interpret exported CSV patterns. Official account, billing, emergency, rebate, conservation, outage, pressure, assistance, and water-quality actions happen on EBMUD's site.

Mud Buddy's baseline, outdoor-watering, and benchmark labels are heuristic pattern clues. They are not official classifications, normalized customer comparisons, leak diagnoses, billing findings, plumbing inspections, or certified conservation measurements.

## What feedback would help

- Is the customer language accurate and safe?
- Are the official EBMUD resource links routed correctly?
- Are any terms too close to official EBMUD program language?
- Would a browser-local CSV helper be useful as a customer education or conservation-support tool?
- What additional disclaimers or methodology notes would EBMUD want before any broader pilot?
