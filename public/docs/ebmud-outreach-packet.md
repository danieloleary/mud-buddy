# EBMUD Outreach Packet

## Short note

```text
Hi,

I built Mud Buddy, a browser-local prototype that helps EBMUD customers interpret their exported usage file.

The goal is customer education and conservation support: help households understand high bills, outdoor watering, normal-use changes, fixture checks, and when to use official EBMUD resources.

The privacy boundary is deliberate:

- no EBMUD login secret;
- no credential automation;
- no CSV upload to a Mud Buddy server;
- no account, billing, autopay, or settings changes;
- no claim of official EBMUD analysis.

I would be grateful for feedback on the customer language, official-resource routing, privacy model, and whether this could be useful as an education or conservation-support pilot.

Live app:
https://danieloleary.github.io/mud-buddy/

Review brief:
https://danieloleary.github.io/mud-buddy/docs/ebmud-review-brief.md

Thank you,
Dan
```

## What Mud Buddy does

Mud Buddy lets a customer upload an EBMUD usage file in the browser and receive a plain-English report:

- normal daily use estimate;
- likely outdoor watering clue;
- highest-use period;
- CSV/data-quality notes;
- recommended homeowner next checks;
- official EBMUD routing for account, billing, emergency, rebate, conservation, outage, pressure, assistance, and water-quality questions.

## What Mud Buddy does not do

- Does not ask for EBMUD credentials.
- Does not automate login, MFA, CAPTCHA, session material, or session material.
- Does not upload usage files to a Mud Buddy backend.
- Does not diagnose leaks as fact.
- Does not decide billing questions.
- Does not replace EBMUD, plumbers, conservation experts, or official emergency channels.
- Does not claim affiliation, approval, endorsement, partnership, or co-branding unless EBMUD authorizes it in writing.

## Feedback ask

Useful EBMUD feedback would include:

- whether the customer language is accurate and safe;
- whether official links are routed correctly;
- whether any terms sound too official or misleading;
- whether the browser-local privacy model is understandable;
- whether an educational or conservation pilot would be useful;
- what disclaimers or methodology notes should change before broader public sharing.

## Suggested review path

1. Try sample report.
2. Review the privacy boundary.
3. Review the report language.
4. Review official EBMUD links.
5. Identify any language that should be softened, removed, or routed to official resources.
